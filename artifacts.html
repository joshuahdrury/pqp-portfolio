Artifact 3: Teacher Coding Game Projects
×

Evidence
All mathematics teachers collaboratively designed and created original coding-based games as a culminating task following multiple PLC sessions focused on developing coding knowledge and instructional practice.

OLF Alignment
Leading the Instructional Program · Building Relationships and Developing People

Reflection
The culminating game design project represented a shift from passive professional learning to active, experiential learning. By positioning teachers as learners, creators, and problem-solvers, the PLC fostered a culture of risk-taking and innovation. Teachers moved beyond initial hesitation with coding to confidently applying computational thinking within mathematics contexts. The collaborative nature of the task reinforced shared ownership of learning and encouraged peer support, allowing teachers to take instructional risks in a supportive environment.

Impact
All teachers successfully implemented coding into their mathematics instruction, significantly increasing integration where it had previously been inconsistent or absent. Teacher confidence, willingness to experiment, and instructional innovation all increased as a result of the PLC process.

Artifact 4: PD Leadership – Securly Classroom
×

Evidence
Designed and facilitated two professional development sessions focused on the implementation and effective use of the Securly Classroom platform.

OLF Alignment
Managing the Organization · Leading the Instructional Program

Reflection
The sessions were intentionally structured to balance technical skill development with practical classroom application. By modelling real classroom scenarios and providing opportunities for guided practice, teachers were able to immediately see the relevance and utility of the platform. Emphasis was placed on building confidence and reducing barriers to adoption, ensuring that all staff felt capable of integrating the tool into their daily practice.

Impact
All middle school teachers are now comfortable using the platform and incorporate it into their practice either daily or as needed, leading to improved classroom management, increased engagement, and more efficient instructional time.

Artifact 6: Student-Led Talent Show
×

Evidence
Supported students in planning, organizing, and hosting a school-wide talent show.

OLF Alignment
Developing the Organization · Building Relationships and Developing People

Reflection
By shifting responsibility to students and acting as a facilitator rather than director, I supported the development of student leadership, collaboration, and ownership. Students were involved in decision-making, logistics, and execution, which strengthened engagement and accountability.

Impact
Increased student voice, leadership capacity, and school community engagement.

Artifact 7: Work-Life Balance

Evidence
Actively model and promote work-life balance through professional practices, boundary-setting, and encouraging staff well-being.

OLF Alignment
Building Relationships and Developing People · Setting Directions

Reflection
Sustainable leadership requires attention to well-being. By modelling balance and encouraging staff to prioritize personal well-being, I contribute to a healthier, more sustainable professional culture. This includes respecting time boundaries, promoting manageable workloads, and recognizing the importance of time outside of work.

Impact
Improved staff morale, reduced burnout, and a more positive and sustainable school culture.
